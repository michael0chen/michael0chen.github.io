X-intercepts

There can be zero, one or two x-intercepts. It is easiest to tell in Standard Form, $y=ax^2+bx+c$.
  • If ${b^2-4ac}>0$, there are two intercepts. (Positive discriminant)
  • If ${b^2-4ac}=0$, there is one intercept. (Zero discriminant)
  • If ${b^2-4ac}<0$, there is no intercept. (Negative discriminant)
FYI, the $b^2-4ac$ part is called the discriminant.
